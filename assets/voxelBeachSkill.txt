Skill: Voxel Beach City Architecture
Goal

Create a voxel beach city that feels believable, varied, and visually interesting while remaining simple enough for efficient asset production.

The city should read as a place people actually live and visit—not as a collection of colored cubes.

Core Philosophy

A good voxel building is remembered by its silhouette, not its window texture.

Favor:

recognizable shapes
layered volumes
strong rooflines
clear purpose
modular construction

Avoid adding tiny voxel noise simply to increase detail.

Evaluation Checklist

A finished voxel beach city should satisfy the following:

✓ Every building has a unique silhouette.
✓ Buildings clearly communicate their function.
✓ Roofs have thickness and overhangs.
✓ Windows vary by building type.
✓ Heights create an interesting skyline.
✓ Streets include props and vegetation.
✓ Trees have multiple silhouettes.
✓ Neighborhoods have distinct identities.
✓ Color palettes are cohesive rather than random.
✓ Most visual richness comes from layered forms and modular details, not excessive voxel density.
Guiding Principle

A memorable voxel city is built from simple, readable forms with purposeful variation. The goal is not to maximize detail, but to maximize character through silhouette, modular composition, and environmental storytelling.
